PE.0202-1 Aquatics

Swimming, Beginner

Show Hide Description

This class is for those who are at ease in shallow water but are not safe in deep water and is designed to teach the non-swimmer the basics of swimming. There will be instruction on floating, gliding, kicking and elementary backstroke. Front crawl stroke will be introduced. Most instruction, particularly in the beginning, takes place in the shallow pool and later may progress to the deep pool as individuals progress. You will be given new goggles to use and keep for swimming. https://physicaleducationandwellness.mit.edu/

Prerequisites: None

Equipment Required: Swim attire needed. Students should bring a filled water bottle and towel. It is recommended to leave your clothing/bags in a locked locker or bring with you on the pool deck.

Waivers Required: None

Health Forms Required: None

PE.0316-1 Dance

Hip Hop

Show Hide Description

Originally known as Street Dance, Hip Hop embodies a large genre of dance that includes freestyle, rhythmic interpretation of music, and social dance norms. This high-energy hip hop class is designed to improve cardiovascular strength while learning innovative ways to move your body to music. A short warm up consisting of stretching and body isolations will be followed by choreography that contains elements of a specific hip hop style. Combinations will be broken down to focus on the energy, musicality, and style of the movement.

Prerequisites: None

Equipment Required: Workout clothes, footwear and water bottle.

Waivers Required: None

Weight Training

Show Hide Description

This course is designed for the beginning or novice weight lifter, or for those who have experience lifting but lack proper instruction. Class will provide students with an understanding of the biomechanics involved, muscles used for a given exercise, and program development. PE.0532-1 Health and Wellness

Fitness (Yoga)/Resiliency (remote synchronous)

Show Hide Description

This wellness course teaches fitness principles, as well as the science and application of resiliency skills. This course will provide students with the permission to take care of themselves in order to maximize energy to reach personal goals and to thrive at MIT and beyond. Each session will consist of half group discussion and half instructional physical activity. Discussion topics will include mindset, positive psychology, mindfulness, gratitude, the power of connection, physical resiliency, and compassion. Physical activity is taught as a way to further develop physical resiliency.

Prerequisites: This remote synchronous course requires students to have the following: Internet access, computer (or tablet, mobile device) with a camera, microphone, and working speaker, MIT Zoom account, roughly 6 foot x 6 foot physical area clear of any objects with a standard 7 - 8 foot ceiling and non-slip floor to do physical activity, comfortable with using 'camera on' function during Zoom sessions

Equipment Required: Yoga mat or towel, comfortable workout clothing and water bottle. Internet access, computer (or tablet, mobile device) with a camera, microphone, and working speaker, MIT Zoom account, comfortable with using 'camera on' function during Zoom sessions. sessions.

Waivers Required: None

Health Forms Required: None

PE.0608-1 Individual Sports

Pistol

Show Hide Description

The P.E. Beginner Pistol course provides an introduction to the basic rules of firearms safety, the "anatomy" of the pistol, how it functions, fundamental sport pistol shooting positions, and the application of safe and effective marksmanship techniques used in the pursuit of sport pistol shooting proficiency.

Please Note: All students will have the opportunity to participate in a beginner-level appropriate competitive match to determine the class "Top Gun." A certificate of accomplishment will be awarded to the Top Gun of each section, and both a certificate and a trophy will be awarded to the overall Top Gun of the Quarter.

Prerequisites: Students must attend first 4 classes, though attendance at all classes is strongly recommended.

Equipment Required: Baseball style hats (old fashioned type with the brim to the front, not the rear), are mandatory. All other equipment will be provided. If students own their own protective shooting glasses and/or hearing protection they may use same after approval by the instructor.

Waivers Required: None

Health Forms Required: None

 TR 11:00 AM Du Pont Pistol Range 14 0 14

Show Hide Description

The P.E. Beginner Rifle course provides an introduction to the basic rules of firearms safety, the "anatomy" of the rifle, how it functions, two fundamental sport rifle shooting positions, and the application of safe and effective marksmanship techniques used in the pursuit of sport rifle shooting proficiency.

Show Hide Description

Pickleball

Show Hide Description

Pickleball is a fast growing racket sport that combines many elements of tennis, badminton, and table tennis. The USAPA currently estimates that there are now over 100,000 players playing pickleball. PE.0720-1 Team Sports

Tchoukball

Show Hide Description

Tchoukball is an indoor, team sport played with a ball, combining elements of ultimate frisbee, basketball and a trampoline. The game is played on a large rectangle court with two half circles at both ends where there is a "frame" that appears to look like a trampoline at a 55 degree angle that you shoot toward. This tramp causes the ball to rebound higher than the original shot. In order to score a point, the ball must be thrown by an attacking player, hit the frame and bounce outside the half circle without being caught by the defending team. Physical contact is prohibited, and defenders may not attempt to intercept the offensive team's passes. Players may take three steps with the ball, hold the ball for a maximum of three seconds, and teams may not pass the ball more than three times before shooting at the frame.

Prerequisites: None

Equipment Required: Court shoes recommended

Waivers Required: None

Health Forms Required: None

PE.0927-1 Outdoor Education

Sailing- Catboat (3 week course)

Show Hide Description

Students will learn to sail catboats with one partner in challenging conditions. Skills include: feathering a boat to windward in strong and gusty breezes, stepping and lowering the mast, learning basic outboard motor skills for getting boat under RR Bridge and through locks, getting away from a mooring and landing at a mooring, safely retrieve a person who falls overboard and anchor selections for types of bottom, breeze and depth. Offered Q2.

Prerequisites: Q2: Must be available Oct: 23, 28, 30 Nov. 4, 6, 13 from 1p-2:30p. Prior sailing experience either with Basic PE&W Sailing or sailing outside of MIT. Participants should not be rank beginners and know how to sail close hauled, tack and gybe a small sailboat. Prior sailing experience in MIT catboats is not required. Students must have completed the swim/boat test by Oct. 15th.

Equipment Required: Synthetic, warm clothing recommended (hats, gloves and/or mittens), spray or water repellent pants are suggested.

Waivers Required: None

Health Forms Required: None
